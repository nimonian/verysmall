Basic integration

Recall from C1 that we may integrate a function $f(x)$ to give a new function $F(x)$.
In symbols, we write: $$ \int f(x) \, dx = F(x) + c $$
Importantly, the derivative of this function $F(x)$ is $f(x)$, so $$ F^{\prime}(x) = f(x) $$

Integrating polynomials

Remember that a polynomial is a function of the form $$ f(x) = a_{n}x^{n} + a_{n-1}x^{n-1} + \ldots + a_{2}x^{2} + a_{1}x + a_{0} $$
The numbers $a_{i}$ are called the coefficients and $a_{0}$ is called the constant term.
The rule for integrating $x^{n}$ is $$ \int x^{n} \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + c$$
