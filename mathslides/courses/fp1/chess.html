Killer Chess Moves

Key ideas for improving your game

About chess
  • There is no formula for a successful chess game
  • ... but there are some heuristics which can help.
  • It is theoretically possible to list every possible chess game - this is why positions are called 'variations'
  • ... but the number of possible games is overwhelmingly large.
  • You must be prepared to lose. A LOT.
Heuristics
  • Don't throw away your pawns
  • Do play the same opening again and again
  • Get your power pieces out on the board quickly... but not always your queen
  • Castle your king in move 6 - 12 on the least active side
  • Loose pieces will be lost
  • If you have a move to spare, reposition your worst piece
Understanding the game
  • Games are roughly broken into the opening, the middle game and the end game
  • At the highest level, players prepare for the endgame during the opening
  • We mortals must make do with not messing up in the first 10 moves
  • The two types of play are tactical and positional
  • Positional play is difficult to fathom and is the reserve of high level players
  • Tactical play is where intermediate players can make the best gains - we will be studying some tactics today

Discovered Threats

When moving one piece, you open up a diagonal or file for another piece to attack. This is especially good if the initial piece can check the king. But if you must trade pieces, make sure you stand to gain!

Attacking the back rank

As the game clears out, it is good to check the back rank of your opponent's army. Is it being covered well enough? Even if you have to sacrifice some pieces, it is all worth it if you can force a check.

Decoys

The most exiting moment in a game of chess is when a piece gets heroically sacrificed - especially the queen! Here, we use pieces as decoys to lure the king to his death. Notice how the king is actually forced to kill before being killed himself... Macbeth, anyone?
